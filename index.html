    Meeting Info

    “The Foundation of the Personal and Professional Skills Employers Seek”
    Guest Speaker:     Olivia Herriford

    Date: Tuesday, May 7th, 4 - 6 PM
    Location: Science 204

    Description

    Integrity, optimistic, trustworthy, empathetic, collaborative, team player, connector, problem solver, and initiative are all characteristics employers use to describe the “attitude” they seek in employees. These are also characteristics of emotional intelligence (EQ). Unlike IQ, EQ develops over time based on how our past experiences shape our thinking and the attention we give to how we interact with others in the present. And EQ can increase your IQ. Olivia will explain how this works and share some personal practices for recognizing, building, and leveraging your EQ to be someone with whom others enjoy working and being around.

    Our Mission

    To prepare students for careers in tech and cyber security through technical and non-technical trainings. To offer focused workshops and trainings on security concepts, soft skills, team and leadership skills. To promote diversity and inclusion. To provide access to job opportunities and more.

    Mission Statement

    The CCSF Cyber Club is a student-run organization at City College of San Francisco. The organization’s stakeholders are currently pursuing various academic studies at City College of San Francisco, leading to certifications, 2-year degrees, 4-year degrees or Master’s degree programs in Computer Science, Computer Network and Information Technology (CNIT), Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Management Information Systems, Business Information Systems, Cyber Security, Networking/Infrastructure, Compliance/Governance, Risk Management, and more.

    The organization’s main purpose is to prepare students for careers in private and public industries. To support students in their academic and career goals, the organization organizes bi-weekly meetings where students are given technical and non-technical workshops to build their professional profiles and opportunities to get involved in teams to support business operations within the CCSF Cyber Club. In addition, the CCSF Cyber Club will partner with the CNIT department, local/international companies and organizations to bring real-world experiences into the hands of students through trainings, community engagement, and job placement.

    Teams

    Competition Team

    The CCSF Cyber Club supports student competition teams who compete in the Western Regional Cyber Collegiate Defense Competition (WRCCDC), Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition (CPTC), and the National Cyber League (NCL), which offers students an opportunity to prepare for employment as security professionals. Students learn to work in teams to secure network infrastructure, develop defensive strategies, and make threat assessments in order to protect business operations from cyber attacks.

    Server Club

    When it comes to programming, do you love developing server side architecture? Or are you curious and want to learn? The CCSF Cyber Club will also support this group to create a space for students who are interested in diving into the server side of programming. Students will have the opportunity to develop server side skills, understand best practices, code-collaboration, and work together as a team on creating projects. Stay tuned for more information coming soon.
